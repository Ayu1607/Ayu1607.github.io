About

I am a PhD scholar in computational solid mechanics at the Department of Civil Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee. My research focuses on the development of thermodynamically consistent constitutive models for damage, plasticity, and fracture in fibre-reinforced polymer composites.

My work combines continuum damage mechanics, phase-field fracture, implicit gradient regularisation, multiscale modelling, and finite element implementation. I am particularly interested in physically grounded and numerically robust models for anisotropic composite failure, damage localisation, and mesh-objective fracture simulation.

Research Vision

My long-term research goal is to develop predictive computational frameworks for composite materials under complex loading conditions. The central idea is to bridge microscale damage mechanisms with structural-scale failure prediction through multiscale modelling, thermodynamic consistency, and robust finite element implementation.

The broader motivation is to improve the reliability of FRP composite structures by developing models that can capture anisotropic damage initiation, plastic deformation, crack propagation, and mode-dependent fracture evolution beyond purely empirical failure envelopes.

Research Areas

Progressive damage in FRP composites

Micromechanics-informed mesoscale damage modelling, mode-wise degradation, and physically motivated failure initiation for anisotropic composites.

Phase-field and gradient regularisation

Phase-field fracture, implicit gradient damage, and regularised damage evolution methods for mesh-objective fracture and damage propagation.

Constitutive modelling

Thermodynamically consistent damage-plasticity coupling, tangent operators, return mapping algorithms, and numerical integration schemes.

Scientific computing

Abaqus UMAT/UEL/VUMAT/VUEL, Fortran, Python, MATLAB, deal.II, postprocessing workflows, and data-driven acceleration for mechanics simulations.
